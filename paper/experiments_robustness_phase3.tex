\section{Robustness: synthetic one-factor ablations (Phase~3A)}
\label{sec:exp_robustness}

We stress the hybrid estimators along single axes of the synthetic generator while keeping all other Phase~0 settings fixed.
Each row in the appendix tables aggregates ten seeds; the main text shows a compact \emph{curve} view at representative knob values and the full $\rho$ sweep for the measurement-ratio axis (where each run fixes one $\rho$).

\paragraph{Protocol.}
Runs are produced by \texttt{python sir\_cs\_pipeline\_optimized.py --profile robustness\_phase3} with \texttt{--robustness-axis} and \texttt{--robustness-value}; the sweep manifest is \texttt{outputs/robustness\_phase3/sweep\_manifest.csv}.

\paragraph{Discussion.}
The measurement-ratio sweep in Table~\ref{tab:robust_measurementratio} isolates very high sampling ratios; at $\rho=0.80$ the classical FISTA hybrid slightly improves over the joint LFISTA model, whereas LFISTA remains clearly better than \texttt{ml\_only}.
Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix_robust_tables} reports analogous RMSE summaries at $\rho=0.60$ for $k$, measurement noise, innovation amplitude, and output noise.

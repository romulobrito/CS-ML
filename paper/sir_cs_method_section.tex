\section{Sparse Innovation Regression with Compressed Sensing (SIR-CS)}
\label{sec:sir_cs}

\subsection{Problem setup}
Let $u \in \mathbb{R}^p$ denote the input variables and let
$y \in \mathbb{R}^N$ be a high-dimensional target.
At inference time, we observe only compressive measurements
\begin{equation}
  b = M y + \eta,
\end{equation}
where $M \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times N}$, with $m \ll N$, is the measurement operator and
$\eta$ models measurement noise.

We decompose the target into a learned background component and an innovation term:
\begin{equation}
  y = f_\theta(u) + r,
\end{equation}
where $f_\theta : \mathbb{R}^p \to \mathbb{R}^N$ is a machine learning predictor and $r$ is the residual.
The main structural assumption is that the residual admits a sparse or compressible representation in a dictionary
$\Psi \in \mathbb{R}^{N \times D}$:
\begin{equation}
  r = \Psi \alpha^\star + \xi,
\end{equation}
where $\alpha^\star \in \mathbb{R}^D$ is sparse (or compressible) and $\xi \in \mathbb{R}^N$
is a small non-sparse mismatch term.

Substituting this decomposition into the measurement model yields
\begin{equation}
  z := b - M f_\theta(u) = A \alpha^\star + \varepsilon,
  \qquad
  A := M \Psi,
  \qquad
  \varepsilon := M \xi + \eta.
\end{equation}
Hence, inference reduces to recovering the innovation coefficients $\alpha^\star$
from the reduced measurements $z$, rather than reconstructing all $N$ coordinates of $y$ directly.

\subsection{Inference: recovery of the innovation}
The basic recovery stage is posed as a penalized sparse regression problem:
\begin{equation}
  \hat{\alpha}
  \in
  \arg\min_{\alpha \in \mathbb{R}^D}
  \frac{1}{2}\|A\alpha - z\|_2^2 + \lambda \|\alpha\|_1.
\end{equation}
The final estimate is then given by
\begin{equation}
  \hat{y} = f_\theta(u) + \Psi \hat{\alpha}.
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Structured variants.}
When support priors, spatial regularity, or domain constraints are available, we consider
\begin{equation}
  \hat{\alpha}
  \in
  \arg\min_{\alpha \in \mathbb{R}^D}
  \frac{1}{2}\|A\alpha - z\|_2^2
  + \lambda_1 \|W\alpha\|_1
  + \lambda_2 \,\Omega_{\mathrm{str}}(\alpha)
  + \lambda_3 \,\mathcal{P}(\Psi\alpha),
  \label{eq:sircs_structured}
\end{equation}
where $W \in \mathbb{R}^{D \times D}$ is a diagonal weight matrix,
$\Omega_{\mathrm{str}}$ encodes structured sparsity
(e.g., group, block, or tree penalties), and $\mathcal{P}$ encodes
domain-specific regularity or physical constraints
(e.g., total variation or physics-based penalties).

The basic estimator is recovered from \eqref{eq:sircs_structured} by taking
$W = I$, $\lambda_2 = 0$, and $\lambda_3 = 0$.

\subsection{Optional learned support prior}
Let $g_\phi : \mathbb{R}^p \to [0,1]^D$ predict support-related scores
\[
  \pi(u) = (\pi_1(u),\dots,\pi_D(u))^\top,
\]
where $\pi_i(u)$ measures the confidence that the $i$th coefficient belongs to the support of $\alpha^\star$.
A simple weighting scheme is
\begin{equation}
  w_i =
  \begin{cases}
    \omega_{\mathrm{low}}, & \pi_i(u) \ge \tau, \\
    1, & \text{otherwise},
  \end{cases}
  \qquad
  0 < \omega_{\mathrm{low}} < 1,
  \quad
  \tau \in (0,1),
\end{equation}
so that
\[
  \|W\alpha\|_1 = \sum_{i=1}^D w_i |\alpha_i|.
\]
This choice penalizes less heavily those coefficients that are deemed more likely to belong to the innovation support.

\subsection{Training objectives}
\paragraph{Supervised training with sparse residual encouragement.}
When full targets $y$ are available during training, one may estimate $\theta$ by minimizing
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{train}}
  =
  \|f_\theta(u) - y\|_2^2
  + \beta \,\|\Psi^\top (y - f_\theta(u))\|_1
  + \gamma \,\Omega_{\mathrm{str}}\!\bigl(\Psi^\top (y - f_\theta(u))\bigr),
\end{equation}
when $\Psi$ is orthonormal and an analysis representation is appropriate.
More generally, $\Psi^\top r$ should be replaced by the analysis operator or latent representation
associated with the chosen dictionary.

\paragraph{Task-aware training with simulated compression.}
Alternatively, one may simulate compressive observations
\[
  b = My + \eta
\]
during training and minimize the post-recovery prediction error
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{task}}
  =
  \|\hat{y}(u,b) - y\|_2^2,
\end{equation}
possibly using unrolled proximal solvers or implicit differentiation when end-to-end differentiability is required.

\subsection{Stability of the recovery stage (consequence of RIP)}
\begin{proposition}[Coefficient and reconstruction stability]
\label{prop:method_recovery_stability}
Let $z=A\alpha^\star+\varepsilon$ with $A=M\Psi$ and $\|\varepsilon\|_2\le \varepsilon_{\mathrm{res}}$.
Let $\widehat{\alpha}$ be any minimizer of $\|\alpha\|_1$ subject to $\|A\alpha-z\|_2\le \varepsilon_{\mathrm{res}}$ (equivalently, a BPDN solution at noise radius $\varepsilon_{\mathrm{res}}$).
Assume $A$ satisfies Assumption~\ref{ass:rip} from Section~\ref{subsec:theory_hybrid_sircs_perspective} (equivalently, \eqref{eq:rip_crt_condition}).
Let $\alpha^\star_k$ be the best $k$-sparse approximation to $\alpha^\star$ obtained by retaining the $k$ largest magnitudes.
Then there exist constants $C_0,C_1>0$ depending only on $(\delta_{3k},\delta_{4k})$ such that
\begin{equation}
  \|\widehat{\alpha}-\alpha^\star\|_2
  \le
  C_0\,\frac{\|\alpha^\star-\alpha^\star_k\|_1}{\sqrt{k}}
  +
  C_1\,\varepsilon_{\mathrm{res}}.
\end{equation}
If $\widehat{y}=f_\theta(u)+\Psi\widehat{\alpha}$ and $y=f^\star(u)+\Psi\alpha^\star+\xi$ with $f^\star$ the unknown background map, then
\begin{equation}
  \|\widehat{y}-y\|_2
  \le
  \|f_\theta(u)-f^\star(u)\|_2 + \|\Psi\|_{2\to 2}\,\|\widehat{\alpha}-\alpha^\star\|_2 + \|\xi\|_2.
\end{equation}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The coefficient bound is Theorem~\ref{thm:stable_sparse_hybrid_residual} applied with $w=\varepsilon$ in \eqref{eq:effective_sparse_problem}; Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix_bpdn_rip_proof} gives the full proof under Assumption~\ref{ass:rip}.
For the reconstruction bound, write
\[
  \widehat{y}-y
  =
  \bigl(f_\theta(u)-f^\star(u)\bigr) + \Psi(\widehat{\alpha}-\alpha^\star) - \xi,
\]
then apply the triangle inequality and the operator norm bound $\|\Psi v\|_2\le \|\Psi\|_{2\to 2}\|v\|_2$.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Algorithm}
\begin{algorithm}
\caption{SIR-CS inference}
\label{alg:sir_cs}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\Require Input $u$, measurements $b$, measurement operator $M$, predictor $f_\theta$, dictionary $\Psi$
\Require Optional support-prior model $g_\phi$
\Require Optional weights $W$, structured penalty $\Omega_{\mathrm{str}}$, and domain penalty $\mathcal{P}$
\State $\hat{y}_{\mathrm{bg}} \gets f_\theta(u)$
\State $z \gets b - M\hat{y}_{\mathrm{bg}}$
\If{a learned support prior is available}
  \State $\pi \gets g_\phi(u)$
  \State Build the diagonal matrix $W = W(\pi)$
\Else
  \State $W \gets I$
\EndIf
\State Solve
\[
  \hat{\alpha}
  \in
  \arg\min_{\alpha \in \mathbb{R}^D}
  \frac{1}{2}\|M\Psi\alpha - z\|_2^2
  + \lambda_1 \|W\alpha\|_1
  + \lambda_2 \Omega_{\mathrm{str}}(\alpha)
  + \lambda_3 \mathcal{P}(\Psi\alpha)
\]
\State $\hat{y} \gets \hat{y}_{\mathrm{bg}} + \Psi \hat{\alpha}$
\Ensure $\hat{y}$
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

\subsection{Method variants}
\textbf{V1 (SIR-CS):} basic sparse recovery with $\ell_1$ regularization on $\alpha$.

\textbf{V2 (WSIR-CS):} weighted sparse recovery using a diagonal matrix $W$ induced by support priors.

\textbf{V3 (TSIR-CS):} temporal regularization on $\alpha_t$, for example through a term such as
$\|\alpha_t - \hat{\alpha}_{t-1}\|_2^2$.

\textbf{V4 (Joint-SIR-CS):} joint recovery with a shared component $\alpha_c$ and channel-specific innovations $\alpha_j$
across multiple outputs or sensing channels.

\subsection{Task-aware unrolled proximal refinement (LFISTA)}
\label{subsec:lfista_method}

The classical recovery step in Section~\ref{sec:sir_cs} uses a generic first-order sparse solver (e.g., FISTA) with fixed iteration schedule and hand-tuned $\lambda$.
An alternative is to \emph{unroll} a fixed number $K$ of proximal-gradient steps on the innovation subproblem
\(
  \min_{\alpha}\tfrac{1}{2}\|A\alpha-z\|_2^2 + \lambda\|\alpha\|_1
\)
with $A=M\Psi$ and $z=b-M f_\theta(u)$, and to treat step sizes and soft-threshold levels as \emph{learnable} per layer.
We denote this block LFISTA (learned FISTA): it maps $z$ to $\hat{\alpha}^{(K)}$ and yields $\hat{y}=f_\theta(u)+\Psi\hat{\alpha}^{(K)}$.
The background $f_\theta$ is a multi-layer perceptron in PyTorch (not identical to the sklearn MLP used for \texttt{ml\_only} in the pipeline).

\paragraph{Training stages (implementation).}
\textbf{(i) Background:} train $f_\theta$ by minimizing $\|f_\theta(u)-y\|_2^2$ on the training set.
\textbf{(ii) Frozen LFISTA:} fix $f_\theta$, train only the unrolled proximal parameters to minimize $\|\hat{y}-y\|_2^2$.
\textbf{(iii) Joint:} fine-tune $f_\theta$ and the LFISTA block together with a smaller learning rate.
This mirrors task-aware objectives in Section~\ref{sec:sir_cs} but replaces the inner convex solver by a differentiable unrolled map.

\subsection{Empirical comparison: LFISTA versus classical hybrid on synthetic data}
\label{subsec:lfista_exp}

We report a controlled benchmark that keeps the Phase~0 data-generating process and the same ten seeds
$\{7,13,23,29,31,37,41,43,47,53\}$ and measurement ratios $\rho\in\{0.20,0.30,0.40,0.50,0.60\}$ as in the main synthetic section.
The pipeline enables \emph{simultaneously}: (a) classical SIR-CS with FISTA on the residual, exposed as \texttt{hybrid\_fista}, with SPGL1 and CS-only variants for context (same dual-solver protocol as Step~1); (b) the PyTorch branch \texttt{ml\_only\_torch}, \texttt{hybrid\_lfista\_frozen}, \texttt{hybrid\_lfista\_joint}.
Thus \texttt{hybrid\_fista} and the LFISTA variants share the same $M$, $\Psi$, splits, and noise model per job; only the estimator class differs.

\paragraph{Reading Table~\ref{tab:lfista_vs_fista_rmse}.}
Both LFISTA variants achieve lower RMSE than \texttt{hybrid\_fista} at every $\rho$, with relative reductions of roughly $5$--$8\%$ versus \texttt{hybrid\_fista} depending on $\rho$.
Frozen and joint estimates are nearly tied at low $\rho$; joint becomes slightly better at $\rho\ge 0.5$, consistent with end-to-end adaptation of $f_\theta$ when measurements are more informative.
Uncertainty (std across seeds) for LFISTA is comparable to or smaller than for \texttt{hybrid\_fista}, so the gap is not explained by higher seed variance.
The PyTorch-only baseline \texttt{ml\_only\_torch} remains above \texttt{hybrid\_fista}, confirming that residual CS (classical or learned) is critical in this regime.

\paragraph{Discussion.}
The synthetic evidence supports two statements aligned with the roadmap: (i) LFISTA improves over a PyTorch background without CS (Figure~\ref{fig:lfista_gains_torch}); (ii) under this protocol, unrolled proximal training can \emph{lower} test RMSE relative to a strong classical FISTA hybrid on the same residual (Table~\ref{tab:lfista_vs_fista_rmse}).
This does not imply superiority on all datasets or measurement models: the comparison omits, for example, identical-$z$ evaluation of classical FISTA against the PyTorch forward (a finer diagnostic left for future work), and computational cost of training is substantially higher than for classical solvers.
